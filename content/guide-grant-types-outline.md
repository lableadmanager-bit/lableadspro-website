# Guide to NIH Grant Types — Content Outline
## For: lableadspro.com/database/guides/grant-types

### Page Purpose
Help life-science sales reps understand what each NIH grant type (activity code) means — and more importantly, which ones signal equipment purchases. Written for reps, not scientists.

---

## Intro (2-3 paragraphs)
Not all grants are created equal. A $3M R01 renewal is a very different sales opportunity than a $150K R21 exploratory grant. This guide breaks down the grant types you'll see in Lab Leads Pro and explains what each one means for your territory.

---

## Section 1: Research Grants (the bread and butter)

### R01 — Research Project Grant
- **What it is:** The gold standard. Multi-year (3-5 years), typically $250K-$500K/year in direct costs. Funds a specific research project.
- **Why it matters for equipment sales:** R01s are where most major equipment purchases happen. Especially in Year 1 (startup buying) and renewal years (upgrading aging equipment). A new R01 in your territory = a warm lead.
- **Typical budget:** $250K-$500K/year direct costs (no cap for most institutes)
- **Duration:** 3-5 years

### R21 — Exploratory/Developmental Research Grant
- **What it is:** Smaller, shorter grants for preliminary/pilot studies. Testing a new idea before going for the R01.
- **Why it matters:** Lower equipment budgets, but signals where a lab is heading. An R21 in single-cell sequencing today often becomes an R01 with a sequencer purchase in 18 months.
- **Typical budget:** Up to $275K total over 2 years
- **Duration:** Up to 2 years

### R03 — Small Research Grant
- **What it is:** Very small grants for discrete, well-defined projects. Common in secondary analysis of existing data.
- **Why it matters:** Rarely leads to equipment purchases. Low priority for prospecting unless the PI also holds larger grants.
- **Typical budget:** Up to $50K/year
- **Duration:** Up to 2 years

### R35 — Outstanding Investigator Award (MIRA)
- **What it is:** Large, flexible, long-term funding for established investigators. Replaces multiple R01s with a single larger award.
- **Why it matters:** Big budgets, long timelines, and flexibility to buy equipment without justifying it to a specific aim. These PIs are well-funded and making purchasing decisions.
- **Typical budget:** $750K/year
- **Duration:** 7 years

### R15 — Academic Research Enhancement Award (AREA)
- **What it is:** Specifically for smaller schools that don't get a lot of NIH funding. Designed to boost research capacity.
- **Why it matters:** These schools are often underequipped. An R15 grant frequently includes equipment line items because the institution doesn't already own what's needed.
- **Typical budget:** Up to $300K over 3 years
- **Duration:** Up to 3 years

## Section 2: SBIR/STTR (Industry & Startups)

### R43 / R44 — SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research)
- **R43 = Phase I** (feasibility, ~$150K over 6-12 months)
- **R44 = Phase II** (full development, up to $1M over 2 years)
- **Why it matters:** Biotech startups building out their first labs. Phase II companies are often purchasing their first major instruments. High-value leads — startups buy fast and don't have legacy vendor relationships.

### R41 / R42 — STTR (Small Business Technology Transfer)
- **Similar to SBIR** but requires a university partner.
- **Why it matters:** Same equipment signals as SBIR. The university partnership sometimes means the equipment goes to the academic lab, sometimes to the company.

## Section 3: Program & Center Grants (Big Money)

### P01 — Research Program Project Grant
- **What it is:** Multi-project grants with shared cores. Large collaborative efforts, often $1M+/year.
- **Why it matters:** P01s fund core facilities and shared equipment. If a P01 includes a microscopy core, that's a potential multi-instrument deal.

### P30 — Center Core Grant
- **What it is:** Supports shared resources and core facilities at an institution.
- **Why it matters:** Directly funds core labs. These are the grants that buy the $500K confocal or the $2M cryo-EM.

### P20 — Exploratory Grants (COBRE/IDeA)
- **What it is:** NIH program to build research capacity at institutions in states that historically receive less NIH funding (IDeA states).
- **Why it matters:** Explicit equipment building. These grants exist to equip labs in underserved states. If you cover an IDeA state (WV, KY, NE, NM, etc.), P20s are gold.

---

## Section 4: Cooperative Agreements

### U01 — Research Project Cooperative Agreement
- **What it is:** Like an R01 but with substantial NIH involvement in the research.
- **Why it matters:** Similar equipment purchasing patterns to R01s. Budgets are often larger due to the collaborative nature.

### U54 — Specialized Center Cooperative Agreement
- **What it is:** Large center-scale cooperative agreements, often multi-institutional.
- **Why it matters:** Large budgets, core facilities, shared equipment purchases. Think of these as P30s with even more NIH involvement.

---

## Section 5: Training & Fellowship Grants (Lower Priority)

### T32 — Institutional Training Grant
- Funds training programs, not equipment. Low priority for prospecting.

### F31 / F32 — Individual Fellowships
- Predoctoral (F31) and postdoctoral (F32) fellowships. These fund people, not labs. Very rarely involve equipment purchases.

### K Awards (K01, K08, K23, K99/R00)
- Career development awards. Small budgets, usually for protected research time. Occasionally include modest equipment needs but not a primary prospecting target.

---

## Section 6: Equipment-Specific Grants

### S10 — Shared Instrumentation Grant
- **What it is:** THE equipment grant. Explicitly funds a single piece of shared equipment costing $50K-$600K.
- **Why it matters:** This is literally a purchase order with NIH funding attached. If an S10 is awarded in your territory, someone is buying exactly one major instrument. The abstract usually names what it is.
- **Typical budget:** $50K-$600K (single instrument)

### S10OD — High-End Instrumentation (HEI) Grant
- **What it is:** The big brother of S10. For instruments costing $600K+.
- **Why it matters:** Cryo-EMs, high-end mass specs, advanced imaging systems. These are the whale deals.
- **Typical budget:** $600K-$2M+

### C06 — Construction Grant
- Funds facility construction/renovation. If a new research building is going up, every lab in it will need equipment.
