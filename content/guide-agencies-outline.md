## Intro (2-3 paragraphs)
NIH gets all the attention, but it's not the only agency funding labs. Federal research spending is spread across 8 major agencies — each with different missions, different types of research, and different buying patterns. Lab Leads Pro tracks all of them so you don't miss leads hiding outside the NIH bubble.

If you're only watching NIH, you're missing roughly 30-40% of federal research funding that touches life-science equipment.

## Agency 1: NIH — National Institutes of Health

### Overview
- **Annual budget:** ~$48B (largest biomedical research funder in the world)
- **What they fund:** Basic and clinical biomedical research across 27 institutes and centers
- **Who gets the money:** Universities, medical schools, research hospitals, some biotech companies

### Why It Matters for Equipment Sales
NIH is the foundation of the lab equipment market. The vast majority of academic research labs in the US are funded at least partially by NIH. Every institute funds research that requires equipment — from microscopes (NIGMS, NEI, NICHD) to sequencers (NHGRI, NCI) to mass specs (NIDDK, NIEHS).

### Key Institutes to Know
- **NCI** (National Cancer Institute) — largest NIH institute, ~$7B/year. Funds imaging, sequencing, flow cytometry, everything.
- **NIAID** (Allergy & Infectious Diseases) — ~$6B. BSL-3/4 labs, advanced imaging, sequencing.
- **NIGMS** (General Medical Sciences) — ~$3B. Broad basic research. Microscopy, structural biology, biochemistry.
- **NHLBI** (Heart, Lung, Blood) — ~$4B. Imaging, physiology equipment, animal facility instrumentation.
- **NINDS** (Neurological Disorders) — ~$2.5B. Electrophysiology, two-photon microscopy, optogenetics rigs.

## Agency 2: NSF — National Science Foundation

### Overview
- **Annual budget:** ~$9B
- **What they fund:** Fundamental research across ALL sciences — not just biomedical. Physics, chemistry, engineering, computer science, earth science, AND biology.
- **Who gets the money:** Primarily universities. Very academic-focused.

### Why It Matters for Equipment Sales
NSF's biological sciences directorate (BIO) funds plant biology, ecology, evolutionary biology, and molecular biology labs that often get overlooked by NIH-focused reps. NSF also has a Major Research Instrumentation (MRI) program — essentially their version of NIH's S10 program — that directly funds equipment purchases.

### Key Directorates
- **BIO** (Biological Sciences) — microscopy, sequencing, environmental chambers
- **MPS** (Math & Physical Sciences) — spectroscopy, mass spec, analytical instruments
- **ENG** (Engineering) — biomedical engineering, materials testing, fabrication equipment
- **MRI Program** — dedicated equipment grants, $100K-$4M. These are purchase orders.

## Agency 3: DOD — Department of Defense

### Overview
- **Research budget:** ~$17B (through DARPA, CDMRP, Army/Navy/Air Force research labs)
- **What they fund:** Defense-relevant research — but this includes a LOT of biomedical and materials science work. Traumatic brain injury, infectious disease, cancer (CDMRP), prosthetics, advanced materials.

### Why It Matters for Equipment Sales
DOD research labs are some of the best-funded in the country, and CDMRP (Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs) funds billions in biomedical research at universities — essentially parallel to NIH but focused on military-relevant health. Labs doing DOD-funded work buy the same microscopes, sequencers, and mass specs as NIH-funded labs.

### Key Programs
- **CDMRP** — Breast Cancer, Prostate Cancer, Neurotrauma, Tick-Borne Disease programs
- **DARPA** — Biological Technologies Office funds cutting-edge biotech
- **Army Research Lab / Naval Research Lab** — materials science, optics, chemistry

## Agency 4: DOE — Department of Energy

### Overview
- **Science budget:** ~$8B (through the Office of Science)
- **What they fund:** Energy research, but also major investments in structural biology, genomics, materials science, and environmental science.

### Why It Matters for Equipment Sales
DOE runs the national labs (Argonne, Brookhaven, Oak Ridge, Lawrence Berkeley, etc.) — massive research facilities with enormous equipment budgets. DOE also funds university research in bioenergy, environmental genomics, and structural biology. Their BER (Biological and Environmental Research) program is a direct overlap with life-science equipment needs.

### Key Programs
- **BER** (Biological & Environmental Research) — genomics, proteomics, imaging
- **BES** (Basic Energy Sciences) — spectroscopy, X-ray, materials characterization
- **National Labs** — these are institutional buyers with dedicated procurement

## Agency 5: NASA

### Overview
- **Research budget:** ~$7B
- **What they fund:** Space biology, astrobiology, earth science, human health in space, materials science.

### Why It Matters for Equipment Sales
NASA-funded labs study the effects of microgravity on cells, grow plants in controlled environments, do advanced imaging of biological samples, and run analytical chemistry on everything from Martian soil analogs to astronaut biospecimens. These are real labs with real equipment budgets — just with cooler mission statements.

### Key Programs
- **Space Biology** — cell culture, microscopy, environmental chambers
- **Human Research Program** — clinical and translational equipment
- **Earth Science** — remote sensing, analytical instruments

## Agency 6: VA — Department of Veterans Affairs

### Overview
- **Research budget:** ~$1.9B
- **What they fund:** Research directly tied to veteran health — PTSD, traumatic brain injury, prosthetics, cancer, infectious disease, aging.

### Why It Matters for Equipment Sales
VA medical centers have research wings that function like university labs. They buy the same equipment, but procurement is federal (which can mean longer sales cycles but more predictable budgets). VA researchers often hold joint appointments at nearby universities.

### Key Areas
- **Biomedical Laboratory R&D** — basic science, same equipment as academic labs
- **Clinical Science R&D** — clinical trials infrastructure
- **Rehabilitation R&D** — prosthetics, assistive tech, engineering equipment

## Agency 7: USDA — Department of Agriculture

### Overview
- **Research budget:** ~$3.5B (through ARS, NIFA, Forest Service)
- **What they fund:** Agricultural research, food safety, animal health, plant biology, environmental science.

### Why It Matters for Equipment Sales
USDA-funded labs do serious molecular biology — genomics, proteomics, microscopy, analytical chemistry. Land-grant universities (every state has one) are major USDA research recipients. These labs need the same sequencers, microscopes, and mass specs as any biomedical lab, but they're studying crops, livestock, and food pathogens instead of human disease.

### Key Programs
- **NIFA** (National Institute of Food & Agriculture) — competitive grants to universities
- **ARS** (Agricultural Research Service) — USDA's own research labs (100+ locations)
- **AFRI** (Agriculture & Food Research Initiative) — NIFA's largest competitive grant program

## Agency 8: CDC — Centers for Disease Control and Prevention

### Overview
- **Extramural research budget:** ~$1B+
- **What they fund:** Public health research, epidemiology, infectious disease surveillance, environmental health, laboratory science.

### Why It Matters for Equipment Sales
CDC funds university and state public health labs working on pathogen surveillance, antimicrobial resistance, environmental monitoring, and diagnostic development. These labs use biosafety equipment, sequencers, PCR systems, mass specs, and microscopes. CDC's own labs in Atlanta are major equipment buyers.

### Key Programs
- **Emerging Infections Programs** — surveillance networks with lab components
- **Advanced Molecular Detection (AMD)** — genomic sequencing for public health
- **Environmental Health Laboratory** — analytical chemistry, toxicology
